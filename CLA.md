## Official Contributions

> [!IMPORTANT]
> ### I Want to Contribute to Marque in an Official *Federal* Government Capacity
>
> If you want to contribute to Marque in an official capacity, meaning:
>
> - **You're a government employee** (i.e. developer) and **want to contribute to Marque while you're at work (or working officially from home)**, or
> - **You're a contractor** on a Federal services contract **and the Government owns your work output under the terms of the contract**
>
> If **you're a contractor** and **aren't sure who owns your work**, talk to your contracting officer (i.e. COR/COTR).
> If you don't fit into any of those two groups, [you need to agree to the CLA](#commercial-and-unofficial-contributions).
> 
> *If you are in one of those two groups, **you can't agree to the CLA***. You can still contribute. If this applies to you, include the following statement with your pull request/contribution:
>   > I make this contribution under my official capacity for the U.S. Government. All included contributions are in the Public Domain.
> 
> That makes it clear that everything in your contribution (and only that) is available for anyone to use however they want -- it's in the Public Domain.
> As part of Marque, Knitli will distribute it under the [Marque License](./LICENSE) and/or other licenses at Knitli's choice (public domain works can be freely relicensed).
> We'll also apply the 'Public Domain' label to your contribution so people can easily find relevant public domain work, like for other government projects.
>
> That's all you need. You can stop reading here.

## For Companies 💼🏢

- If you’re contributing for your employer, your *company* or organization needs to agree to your contributions. You can do this in a few ways:

  - Attach a note to your contribution from someone who can agree to things for the company expressly saying you, and preferably anyone from your company, can contribute to Marque under these terms. Something like: 

      ```text
      I authorize ACME Corp employees to contribute to Marque as part of their work for ACME. ACME understands the terms of Marque's Contributor License Agreement, and agrees to its terms within the scope of our employees' official contributions to the project.

      Jane Decisionmaker
      Corporate Counsel, ACME Corp
      ```

  - Link or include a copy of an official corporate policy providing guidance on CLA contributions, along with any materials needed to show you're in compliance with the policy's terms. 
  - Have your company [send us a note](mailto:licensing@knitli.com) providing permission, such as one like the note above.
  - If you can act on behalf of the company (i.e. authorized executive or board member) then provide a copy of an official document indicating that role or other evidence of your position (a link to an official about page with your smiling face is fine) and agree to the CLA on behalf of your company.
