# Bibliography — marque-lattice-consultant skill

Organized by topic. Within each topic, entries are sorted alphabetically by citation key.

## Pure order theory & lattice algebra

`[abramsky-jung-handbook]`
Abramsky, S. & Jung, A. (1994). "Domain Theory." Chapter 3 of *Handbook of Logic in Computer Science*, Vol. 3 (S. Abramsky, D. M. Gabbay, T. S. E. Maibaum, eds.). Oxford University Press.
Corrected and expanded version freely available: https://www.cs.ox.ac.uk/files/298/handbook.pdf
The standard reference for dcpos, Scott topology, and continuous domains.

`[birkhoff-1937]`
Birkhoff, G. (1937). "Rings of sets." Duke Mathematical Journal, 3(3): 443–454.
DOI: 10.1215/S0012-7094-37-00334-X. Paywalled.
Original publication of Birkhoff's representation theorem for finite distributive lattices and the M3-N5 forbidden-sublattice characterization.

`[birkhoff-1967]`
Birkhoff, G. *Lattice Theory*, 3rd edition. American Mathematical Society Colloquium Publications, Volume 25. AMS, 1967. ISBN 978-0821810255.
Publisher: https://bookstore.ams.org/coll-25/
A 1948 (2nd) edition PDF circulates academically: https://math.chapman.edu/~jipsen/summerschool/Birkhoff%201948%20Lattice%20Theory%20Revised%20Edition.pdf
Foundational treatise, in print since 1940 (1st ed.). Cited for ch. I §6 (lattice axioms), §7 (modular and distributive lattices).

`[crawley-dilworth-1973]`
Crawley, P. & Dilworth, R. P. *Algebraic Theory of Lattices*. Prentice-Hall, 1973. ISBN 978-0130222695.
Out of print; loanable scan via Internet Archive: https://archive.org/details/algebraictheoryo0000craw

`[davey-priestley-2002]`
Davey, B. A. & Priestley, H. A. *Introduction to Lattices and Order*, 2nd edition. Cambridge University Press, 2002. ISBN 978-0521784511.
DOI: 10.1017/CBO9780511809088. Paywalled.
Publisher: https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/introduction-to-lattices-and-order/946458CB6638AF86D85BA00F5787F4F4
Internet Archive (loanable scan, 1st ed. 1990): https://archive.org/details/introductiontola0000dave
The default reference text in lattice theory; cited heavily throughout. Specific theorem numbers given in catalog entries reference the 2nd edition.

`[dedekind-1894]`
Dedekind, R. (1894). "Über die von drei Moduln erzeugte Dualgruppe." Mathematische Annalen, 53: 371–403. (German.)
DOI: 10.1007/BF01448979.
Original paper introducing modular lattices ("Dualgruppen") and Dedekind's modular law.

`[dilworth-1950]`
Dilworth, R. P. (1950). "A Decomposition Theorem for Partially Ordered Sets." Annals of Mathematics (2), 51(1): 161–166.
DOI: 10.2307/1969503. Paywalled (JSTOR).
Original statement of Dilworth's theorem on chain decomposition.

`[freese-nation-lectures]`
Freese, R. & Nation, J. B. *Lectures on Free Lattices*, in Proceedings of the Hawaii Conference on Lattices, Algebras and Computation (1995); also Springer 1996.
Author hosts open expository version: https://math.hawaii.edu/~ralph/Classes/649M/freelat.pdf
Detailed treatment of Whitman's algorithm.

`[gratzer-2011]`
Grätzer, G. *Lattice Theory: Foundation*. Birkhäuser/Springer, 2011. ISBN 978-3-0348-0017-4.
DOI: 10.1007/978-3-0348-0018-1. Paywalled.
Comprehensive modern treatise; ch. III on congruences is a particularly detailed reference.

`[hales-1964]`
Hales, A. W. (1964). "On the non-existence of free complete Boolean algebras." Fundamenta Mathematicae, 54: 45–66.
DOI: 10.4064/fm-54-1-45-66. Open access via Polish Academy of Sciences.
Cross-referenced from pure-lattice.md entry 10 (free lattice; cardinality remarks for free *complete* structures).

`[johnstone-stone-spaces]`
Johnstone, P. T. *Stone Spaces*. Cambridge Studies in Advanced Mathematics 3. Cambridge University Press, 1982. ISBN 978-0521337793.
DOI: 10.1017/CBO9781139161512.
Stone duality, distributive lattices, frames, Heyting algebras at sheaf-theoretic depth. Also cited in `frames-locales.md`.

`[mirsky-1971]`
Mirsky, L. (1971). "A Dual of Dilworth's Decomposition Theorem." American Mathematical Monthly, 78(8): 876–877.
DOI: 10.2307/2316481. Paywalled (JSTOR).

`[nation-lattice-notes]`
Nation, J. B. *Notes on Lattice Theory*. University of Hawaii. https://math.hawaii.edu/~jb/math618/Nation-LatticeTheory.pdf
Available on author's institutional homepage; no explicit license. Comprehensive open lecture notes covering posets, semilattices, lattices, modular and distributive lattices, free lattices, congruences, varieties.

`[ore-1944]`
Ore, Ø. (1944). "Galois Connexions." Transactions of the American Mathematical Society, 55: 493–513.
DOI: 10.2307/1990305. Paywalled (JSTOR).
The originating paper coining the term "Galois connection" (in antitone form). Also cited under Galois-connections topic.

`[stone-1936]`
Stone, M. H. (1936). "The Theory of Representations of Boolean Algebras." Transactions of the American Mathematical Society, 40: 37–111.
DOI: 10.2307/1989664. Paywalled (JSTOR).
Stone's representation theorem for Boolean algebras as fields of clopen sets.

`[tarski-1955]`
Tarski, A. (1955). "A lattice-theoretical fixpoint theorem and its applications." Pacific Journal of Mathematics, 5(2): 285–309.
DOI: 10.2140/pjm.1955.5.285.
Open access via Project Euclid: https://projecteuclid.org/euclid.pjm/1103044538
The canonical statement of the Knaster-Tarski theorem in the form used by modern textbooks.

`[whitman-1941a]`
Whitman, P. M. (1941). "Free Lattices." Annals of Mathematics (2), 42: 325–329.
DOI: 10.2307/1969001. Paywalled (JSTOR).
Original solution to the word problem for free lattices.

`[whitman-1941b]`
Whitman, P. M. (1942). "Free Lattices II." Annals of Mathematics (2), 43: 104–115.
DOI: 10.2307/1968883. Paywalled (JSTOR).
Continuation of the free-lattice word problem; defines what is now known as Whitman's condition.

## Universal algebra & varieties

`[bergman-2011]`
Bergman, C. *Universal Algebra: Fundamentals and Selected Topics*. Pure and Applied Mathematics. Chapman & Hall / CRC, 2011. ISBN 978-1439851296.
Publisher: https://www.routledge.com/Universal-Algebra-Fundamentals-and-Selected-Topics/Bergman/p/book/9781439851296
A standard graduate text on universal algebra; cited in `universal-algebra.md` for variety theory, quasi-varieties, ultraproducts, and many-sorted algebra. Paywalled.

`[birkhoff-1935]`
Birkhoff, G. (1935). "On the structure of abstract algebras." Mathematical Proceedings of the Cambridge Philosophical Society, 31(4): 433–454.
DOI: 10.1017/S0305004100013463. Paywalled.
The original variety theorem (HSP characterization); cited in `universal-algebra.md` entry 4. Tarski's later restatement in `[burris-sankappanavar-1981]` Theorem II.9.5 is the form most often cited today.

`[burris-sankappanavar-1981]`
Burris, S. & Sankappanavar, H. P. *A Course in Universal Algebra*. Springer-Verlag, 1981. The Millennium Edition (2012) is freely distributed by the authors.
Open-access PDF: https://www.math.uwaterloo.ca/~snburris/htdocs/UALG/univ-algebra2012.pdf
Vendored at: `sources/burris-sankappanavar-universal-algebra.pdf` (4.4 MB).
Lattice theory is treated in §I.3–I.4; congruences in §II.5; coproducts and free algebras in §II.4 and §II.10. The Foreword of the Millennium Edition explicitly authorizes free distribution.

`[goguen-meseguer-1992]`
Goguen, J. A. & Meseguer, J. (1992). "Order-Sorted Algebra I: Equational Deduction for Multiple Inheritance, Overloading, Exceptions, and Partial Operations." Theoretical Computer Science, 105(2): 217–273.
DOI: 10.1016/0304-3975(92)90302-V. Paywalled (Elsevier).
Foundational paper on order-sorted algebra; cited in `universal-algebra.md` entry 18 for typed/subsort algebraic structure.

`[gratzer-1979]`
Grätzer, G. *Universal Algebra*, 2nd ed. Springer, 1979 (1st ed. 1968). Reprinted 2008 with updates: ISBN 978-0387774862.
DOI: 10.1007/978-0-387-77487-9. Paywalled.
Publisher: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-0-387-77487-9
The foundational reference for universal algebra; cited in `universal-algebra.md` for partial-algebra theory, polynomial vs term functions, and free algebra constructions.

`[mckenzie-mcnulty-taylor-1987]`
McKenzie, R., McNulty, G. F., & Taylor, W. F. *Algebras, Lattices, Varieties*, Volume I. Wadsworth & Brooks/Cole, 1987 (reprinted by AMS Chelsea, 2018, ISBN 978-1470442958).
Publisher (AMS reprint): https://bookstore.ams.org/chel-383
The deepest contemporary treatment of variety theory and tame congruence theory. Cited as supporting reference in `universal-algebra.md` entry 4 (HSP theorem).

`[meinke-tucker-1992]`
Meinke, K. & Tucker, J. V. (1992). "Universal Algebra." In *Handbook of Logic in Computer Science*, Vol. 1 (Background: Mathematical Structures), eds. S. Abramsky, D. M. Gabbay, T. S. E. Maibaum. Oxford University Press.
Publisher: https://global.oup.com/academic/product/handbook-of-logic-in-computer-science-9780198537618
Introductory chapter cited in `universal-algebra.md` entry 18 as a many-sorted algebra primer.

## Frames, locales, pointless topology

`[picado-pultr-2012]`
Picado, J. & Pultr, A. *Frames and Locales: Topology Without Points*. Frontiers in Mathematics. Birkhäuser/Springer, 2012. ISBN 978-3-0348-0153-9 (softcover), 978-3-0348-0154-6 (eBook).
DOI: 10.1007/978-3-0348-0154-6. Paywalled.
Publisher: https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-0348-0154-6
The standard modern textbook on point-free topology. Cited heavily in `frames-locales.md` for definitions of frame, locale, sublocale, nucleus, and the duality between them.

`[vickers-1989]`
Vickers, S. *Topology via Logic*. Cambridge Tracts in Theoretical Computer Science 5. Cambridge University Press, 1989. ISBN 978-0521360623 (hardcover), 978-0521576512 (paperback). xiii + 200 pp.
DOI: 10.1017/CBO9781139087414 (digital edition).
Internet Archive (loanable scan): https://archive.org/details/topologyvialogic0000stev
Locale-theoretic introduction motivated by the logic of finite observations. Cited in `frames-locales.md` for the philosophical entry-point to point-free topology and policy-as-frame framing.

(Cross-reference: `[johnstone-stone-spaces]` listed under "Pure order theory & lattice algebra" — also a primary frames/locales reference.)

`[erne-koslowski-melton-strecker-1993]`
Erné, M., Koslowski, J., Melton, A., & Strecker, G. E. (1993). *A Primer on Galois Connections*. Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences, 704: 103–125.
DOI: 10.1111/j.1749-6632.1993.tb52513.x
Author's preprint version (PostScript): http://www.math.ksu.edu/~strecker/primer.ps
Vendored as PDF (converted via ps2pdf) at: `sources/erne-koslowski-melton-strecker-galois-primer.pdf` (≈350 KB) plus the original PS at `sources/erne-koslowski-melton-strecker-galois-primer.ps`.

## Domain theory & fixed-point theorems

`[kleene-1952]`
Kleene, S. C. *Introduction to Metamathematics*. North-Holland, 1952.
ISBN 0-7204-2103-9. In-print reissues by Ishi Press (2009).
The original presentation of the Kleene fixed-point theorem (the "first recursion theorem" in computability-theory form). Cited via Møller-Schwartzbach 2025 §4.4.

## Abstract interpretation

`[bagnara-hill-zaffanella-2005]`
Bagnara, R., Hill, P. M., & Zaffanella, E. (2005). "The Parma Polyhedra Library." Science of Computer Programming 72(1–2): 3–21.
DOI: 10.1016/j.scico.2007.08.001. Paywalled.
Open-access preprint: https://arxiv.org/abs/cs/0612085
The standard open-source library implementing polyhedra and various weakly-relational domains.

`[bourdoncle-1993]`
Bourdoncle, F. (1993). "Efficient chaotic iteration strategies with widenings." In *Formal Methods in Programming and Their Applications*, LNCS 735, Springer, pp. 128–141.
DOI: 10.1007/BFb0039704. Paywalled.
Introduces "weak topological orderings" for guiding chaotic iteration; standard reference for industrial AI implementation.

`[cousot-2014]`
Cousot, P. (2014). *Abstract Interpretation: Achievements and Challenges*. POPL '14 invited talk.
Author copy: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/Cousot-POPL-2014-ACM-p2--3-2014.pdf
Modern survey of the AI framework, including domain composition, completeness, and the state of practice as of mid-2010s.

`[cousot-2021-book]`
Cousot, P. *Principles of Abstract Interpretation*. MIT Press, 2021. ISBN 978-0262044905.
Publisher: https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262044905/principles-of-abstract-interpretation/
The recent comprehensive treatment by the originator of the framework. Out-of-scope-but-canonical reference.

`[cousot-cousot-1976]`
Cousot, P. & Cousot, R. (1976). "Static determination of dynamic properties of programs." In *Proceedings of the 2nd International Symposium on Programming*, B. Robinet (ed.), Dunod, Paris, pp. 106–130.
Author copy: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/CousotCousot-ISOP-76-Dunod-p106--130-1976.pdf
The earliest published statement of the interval abstract domain, predating the canonical 1977 framework paper.

`[cousot-cousot-1977]`
Cousot, P. & Cousot, R. (1977). *Abstract Interpretation: A Unified Lattice Model for Static Analysis of Programs by Construction or Approximation of Fixpoints*. Conference Record of the 4th ACM Symposium on Principles of Programming Languages (POPL '77), pp. 238–252. ACM Press.
DOI: 10.1145/512950.512973. Paywalled at ACM.
Author preprint: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/CousotCousot-POPL-77-ACM-p238--252-1977.pdf
Vendored at: `sources/cousot-cousot-popl77.pdf` (940 KB).
License note: ACM-copyrighted. Author institutional preprint; treat as fair-academic-citation use, *not* free redistribution. The originating paper of the abstract-interpretation framework. Defines Galois connections in the AI sense, soundness, widening, narrowing, the constructive lfp approach.

`[cousot-cousot-1979]`
Cousot, P. & Cousot, R. (1979). *Systematic Design of Program Analysis Frameworks*. Conference Record of the 6th ACM Symposium on Principles of Programming Languages (POPL '79), pp. 269–282.
DOI: 10.1145/567752.567778. Paywalled at ACM.
Author preprint: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/CousotCousot-POPL-79-ACM-p269--282-1979.pdf
Vendored at: `sources/cousot-cousot-popl79.pdf` (1.1 MB).
License note: same as above — ACM-copyrighted; author institutional preprint; fair academic use only.
Introduces Galois insertion (`α∘γ = id`), reduced product, the systematic design recipe deriving `f^♯` from `f` and `(α, γ)`, and the relationship between Galois connections and approximation operators.

`[cousot-cousot-1992]`
Cousot, P. & Cousot, R. (1992). "Comparing the Galois Connection and Widening/Narrowing Approaches to Abstract Interpretation." In *Programming Language Implementation and Logic Programming* (PLILP '92), LNCS 631, Springer, pp. 269–295.
DOI: 10.1007/3-540-55844-6_142. Paywalled (Springer).
Author copy: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/CousotCousot-PLILP-92-LNCS-v631-p269--295-1992.pdf
Compares the strict-Galois-connection approach (no widening) against the widening/narrowing approach. Resolves several confusion points around what each formalism buys.

`[cousot-cousot-2012]`
Cousot, P. & Cousot, R. (2012). "An Abstract Interpretation Framework for Termination." In *POPL '12*, ACM, pp. 245–258.
DOI: 10.1145/2103656.2103687. Paywalled.
Author copy: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/CousotCousot-POPL-2012-ACM-p245--258-2012.pdf
The "termination as abstract interpretation" line — treats termination itself as a fixed-point question on an auxiliary lattice. Cited for completeness; out of scope for typical marque shapes.

`[cousot-halbwachs-1978]`
Cousot, P. & Halbwachs, N. (1978). "Automatic Discovery of Linear Restraints Among Variables of a Program." In *POPL '78*, ACM, pp. 84–96.
DOI: 10.1145/512760.512770. Paywalled.
Author copy: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/CousotHalbwachs-POPL-78-ACM-p84--97-1978.pdf
The canonical introduction of the polyhedral abstract domain. Original algorithms for join (convex hull) and widening on polyhedra.

`[cousot-monerau-2012]`
Cousot, P. & Monerau, M. (2012). "Probabilistic Abstract Interpretation." In *European Symposium on Programming* (ESOP '12), LNCS 7211, Springer, pp. 169–193.
DOI: 10.1007/978-3-642-28869-2_9. Paywalled.
Author copy: https://www.di.ens.fr/~cousot/COUSOTpapers/publications.www/CousotMonerau-ESOP-2012-LNCS-v7211-p169--193-2012.pdf
Abstract domains over probability distributions. Cross-referenced from `abstract-interp.md` entry 19 (confidence propagation).

`[di-pierro-wiklicky-2000]`
Di Pierro, A. & Wiklicky, H. (2000). "Concurrent Constraint Programming: Towards Probabilistic Abstract Interpretation." In *Principles and Practice of Declarative Programming* (PPDP '00), pp. 127–138.
DOI: 10.1145/351268.351284. Paywalled.

`[giacobazzi-quintarelli-2001]`
Giacobazzi, R. & Quintarelli, E. (2001). "Incompleteness, Counterexamples and Refinements in Abstract Model Checking." In *Static Analysis Symposium* (SAS '01), LNCS 2126, Springer, pp. 356–373.
DOI: 10.1007/3-540-47764-0_20. Paywalled.
Formal taxonomy of completeness defects in AI: forward-incompleteness, backward-incompleteness.

`[kam-ullman-1977]`
Kam, J. B. & Ullman, J. D. (1977). "Monotone data flow analysis frameworks." Acta Informatica 7(3): 305–317.
DOI: 10.1007/BF00290339. Paywalled (Springer).
Generalizes Kildall to the monotone (non-distributive) case; proves MOP < MFP gap in general.

`[kildall-1973]`
Kildall, G. A. (1973). "A unified approach to global program optimization." In *POPL '73*, ACM, pp. 194–206.
DOI: 10.1145/512927.512945. Paywalled.
Internet Archive (open scan of conference proceedings): https://archive.org/details/popl73-proceedings
The originating paper for the monotone framework + worklist algorithm; assumes distributive transfer functions.

`[knuth-1973]`
Knuth, D. E. *The Art of Computer Programming*, Volume 1: *Fundamental Algorithms*, 3rd edition. Addison-Wesley, 1997 (original 1968; 3rd ed. 1997, here cited as "1973" reflecting the historical second edition).
ISBN 978-0201896831. In print.
§2.2.3 is the canonical reference for topological sort; cited from `abstract-interp.md` entry 18.

`[mine-2001]`
Miné, A. (2001). "The Octagon Abstract Domain." In *Working Conference on Reverse Engineering* (WCRE '01), IEEE, pp. 310–319.
DOI: 10.1109/WCRE.2001.957836. Paywalled.
Author copy: https://www-apr.lip6.fr/~mine/publi/article-mine-ast01.pdf
Introduces the octagon domain; a polynomial-cost relational refinement of intervals.

`[mine-2006]`
Miné, A. (2006). "The Octagon Abstract Domain." Higher-Order and Symbolic Computation 19(1): 31–100.
DOI: 10.1007/s10990-006-8609-1. Paywalled.
Author copy: https://www-apr.lip6.fr/~mine/publi/article-mine-HOSC06.pdf
Journal-length elaboration of the octagon domain.

`[moller-schwartzbach-spa]`
Møller, A. & Schwartzbach, M. I. *Static Program Analysis*. Lecture notes, Aarhus University, edition of April 29, 2025.
Open-access PDF: https://cs.au.dk/~amoeller/spa/spa.pdf
License: Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives 4.0 International (CC-BY-NC-ND 4.0).
Vendored at: `sources/moller-schwartzbach-static-analysis.pdf` (1.2 MB).
The standard didactic reference for monotone framework data-flow analysis, lattice theory for AI, sign / constant-propagation / interval domains, widening, and Galois connections at textbook depth. Chapters 4 (Lattice Theory), 5 (Dataflow Analysis with Monotone Frameworks), 6 (Widening), and 12 (Soundness, Galois Connections) are referenced extensively.

`[monniaux-2001]`
Monniaux, D. (2001). "An abstract analysis of the probabilistic logical semantics of an imperative programming language." Pacific Symposium on Biocomputing.
Author copy at https://www-verimag.imag.fr/~monniaux/.
Early probabilistic AI; cross-referenced from `abstract-interp.md` entry 19.

`[muchnick-1997]`
Muchnick, S. S. *Advanced Compiler Design and Implementation*. Morgan Kaufmann, 1997. ISBN 978-1558603202.
Cited for the accessible exposition of MOP vs. MFP and Kildall's algorithm in §8.

`[nielson-nielson-hankin-1999]`
Nielson, F., Nielson, H. R., & Hankin, C. *Principles of Program Analysis*. Springer, 1999. Corrected 2nd printing 2005. ISBN 978-3540654100.
DOI: 10.1007/978-3-662-03811-6. Paywalled (book).
Comprehensive textbook covering data-flow analysis, control-flow analysis, AI, and type-and-effect systems. Standard graduate-level reference.

`[ranzato-tapparo-2007]`
Ranzato, F. & Tapparo, F. (2007). "Strong preservation as completeness in abstract interpretation." In *European Symposium on Programming* (ESOP '07).
DOI: 10.1007/978-3-540-71316-6_2. Paywalled.
Refinement of the completeness theory.

## Security & information-flow lattices

`[anderson-securityengineering]`
Anderson, R. *Security Engineering: A Guide to Building Dependable Distributed Systems*. Wiley.
1st edition (2001) freely available: https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~rja14/book.html
2nd edition (2008), 3rd edition (2020) paywalled.
Open chapter (2nd ed. Ch. 9 "Multilateral Security"): https://www.cl.cam.ac.uk/~rja14/Papers/SEv2-c09.pdf
Definitive textbook treatment of compartmentation, lattice models, BMA medical-records model, Chinese Wall.

`[bell-2005-looking-back]`
Bell, D. E. (2005). "Looking Back at the Bell-La Padula Model." Proceedings of the 21st Annual Computer Security Applications Conference (ACSAC).
DOI: 10.1109/CSAC.2005.37. Paywalled on IEEE.
Open author archive (image-PDF): https://www.acsac.org/2005/papers/Bell.pdf
Bell's retrospective essay. Discusses the boolean-lattice generalization, CANUKUS coalition policies, and lessons for multilateral security policies.

`[bell-lapadula-1973]`
Bell, D. E. & LaPadula, L. J. (1973). *Secure Computer Systems: Mathematical Foundations*. MITRE Technical Report MTR-2547, Vol. I (also DTIC AD-770768).
Public-domain DTIC document.
DTIC: https://apps.dtic.mil/sti/citations/AD0770768
Author archive (image-PDF): https://lukemuehlhauser.com/wp-content/uploads/Bell-LaPadula-Secure-computer-systems-mathematical-foundations.pdf
Internet Archive text version: https://archive.org/stream/DTIC_AD0770768/DTIC_AD0770768_djvu.txt
The original Bell-LaPadula model. Vol. I covers mathematical foundations (state machines, simple-security and ⋆-property formalization). Vol. II–IV cover refinements and Multics interpretation.

`[bell-lapadula-1975-unified]`
Bell, D. E. & LaPadula, L. J. (1975). *Secure Computer Systems: Unified Exposition and Multics Interpretation*. MITRE Technical Report MTR-2997 (also ESD-TR-75-306).
Public-domain DTIC document.
The unified version with the Basic Security Theorem and Schiller's "current-security-level" simplification of ⋆-property.

`[biba-1977]`
Biba, K. J. (1977). *Integrity Considerations for Secure Computer Systems*. MITRE Technical Report MTR-3153 (also USAF Electronic Systems Division ESD-TR-76-372 / NTIS AD-A039324).
Public-domain DTIC document.
CERIAS reference page: https://www.cerias.purdue.edu/apps/reports_and_papers/view/2834
Original Biba integrity model: Strict Integrity, Subject Low-Water-Mark, Object Low-Water-Mark, Ring policies. Strict Integrity is "the Biba model" in colloquial use.

`[bishop-2018]`
Bishop, M. *Computer Security: Art and Science*, 2nd edition. Addison-Wesley, 2018.
ISBN 978-0321712332. Paywalled.
Publisher: https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/computer-security-art/9780134097145/
Chapter 5: information flow; Appendix 34: lattice background. Standard graduate textbook.

`[brewer-nash-1989]`
Brewer, D. F. C. & Nash, M. J. (1989). "The Chinese Wall Security Policy." Proceedings of the 1989 IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy, pp. 206–214.
DOI: 10.1109/SECPRI.1989.36295. Paywalled.
Open archives: https://www.cs.purdue.edu/homes/ninghui/readings/AccessControl/brewer_nash_89.pdf, https://www.cs.nmt.edu/~doshin/t/s06/cs589/pub/4.BN-ChWall.pdf
The original Chinese Wall model, with the explicit claim that CW "cannot be correctly represented by a Bell-LaPadula model."

`[csl-rushby-blp]`
Rushby, J. (1986). "The Bell and La Padula Security Model." SRI CSL Technical Note.
Open access: https://www.csl.sri.com/~rushby/papers/blp86.pdf
